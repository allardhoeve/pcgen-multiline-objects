PCGen's 4E Data Set Creation: Equipment

This lesson is divided into two sections; an explanation of the tags used in an equipment.lst file, and how to build equipment for a 4e style game.

Equipment File Tags

The <equipment name> and TYPE are required tags in any equipment line. All other tags are optional. You will find below a list of the most common tags to build equipment of any type:

<equipment name>

Equipment lines always begin with the name of the piece of eqipment being created.

TYPE

Determines what "type" of item this is to be categorized as. Common types of equipment found in the RSRD are; Weapon, Armor, Shield, Ring, Glove, Bracer, Headgear, Eyewear, Amulet, etc. In the case of weapons, armor and shields, there as few additional standard types used in the RSRD include Natural, Heavy, Light, Medium, Melee, Ranged, Slashing, Piercing and Bludgeoning.

Certain Types will show up in different locations on the character sheet, or will determine whether an item can just be "equipped" or goes in a specific location. For more information you can take a look at the Global TYPE: Equipment page.

ACCHECK

Used for Armor or Shields, determines what the penalty of the item bestows for certain skills, and other formulas dependent upon the penalty. (number is done '-x')

ALTCRITMULT

Useful for double weapons. Determines what the alternate head has for it's critical multiplier. '-' is none, otherwise 'xy' with 'y' being the number is common.

ALTCRITRANGE

What is the die range for criticals on the alternate head of a double weapon. 1 = 20, 2 = 19 & 20, etc.

ALTEQMOD

What equipment modifiers are applied to the alternate head of a double weapon

ALTTYPE

Determines the 'types' for the alternate head

CRITMULT

Same function as ALTCRITMULT only it is used for either the primary head or non-double weapons

CRITRANGE

Determines the critical die range for the primary weapon

DAMAGE

Displays the damage this item can do. (Normally used for Weapons)

FUMBLERANGE

Other Useful Tags

An additional tag that can be used in the equipment file is the Global BONUS tag. <blahblahblah>

When coding items, we tend to only use bonus tags for constant bonuses, for non-constant or changing bonuses we leave those out or place them in the SPROP tag, however, you may also apply those bonuses using the PREAPPLY tag, allowing it as a temporary modifier.

Building Equipment for a 4th Edition Style Game

Sample Items you might make

The Monkey War Hammer of Luck +3. This +3 Great War Hammer imbues great coding skill as you bash the computer into submission. It deals an additional 2d6 on a hit. It can be thrown 10 squares. Reach of 10 ft. One Handed weapon for a Large Creature, weighs 25 pounds.

Now that we have a simple grasp of items let's make something, Let's do a Weapon, a piece of armor and a Shield.

Sample Weapon:

Monkey War Hammer (Of Luck)

OUTPUTNAME:Monkey War Hammer of Luck +2

PROFICIENCY:WEAPON|War Hammer

TYPE:Weapon.Melee.Ranged.Thrown.Bludgeoning.Hammer.Warhammer.AdvancedMelee.MeleeBludgeoning.RangedBludgeoning

DAMAGE:2d6

CRITRANGE:1

BONUS:WEAPON|TOHIT,DAMAGE|3|TYPE=Enhancement

NOTE: You can obtain the same result using eqmods. (Example: EQMOD:PLUS3W.SpecialArtifactFooBar) You can also place special unseen abilities on items not available to players as choices. Useful if you want an artifact or a special item.

RANGE:10

REACH:10

SIZE:L

WIELD:OneHanded

WT:25

SPROP:Monkey War Hammer of Luck +3 imbues great coding skill as you bash the computer into submission.

This is a nasty weapon, it deals 2d6 on a hit, plus an enhancement bonus of plus 3. It has an eqmod that is either granting it something else (we don't know unless we look at it or it has an SPROP tag), we can throw it in increments of 10, it has a reach of 10 ft, and it's a OneHanded weapon for a Large Creature, weighing in at an impressive 25 pounds.

Sample Armor:

Monkey Dream Armor

TYPE:Armor.Heavy.Suit.Unique

PROFICIENCY:ARMOR|Heavy

BONUS:COMBAT|AC|8|TYPE=Armor.REPLACE

BONUS:MOVEADD|TYPE.Walk|-1

ACCHECK:-2

COST:50

WT:50

Sample Shield

Banana Shield

PROFICIENCY:SHIELD|Light Shield

TYPE:Shield.Light

COST:1

WT:10

BONUS:COMBAT|AC|1|TYPE=Shield

Remember, the Proficiency name used must have a corresponding name in the correct file (Armor Prof, Weapon Prof or Shield Prof file).
